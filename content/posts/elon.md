The Book **"Elon Musk - How the Billioanire CEO of SpaceX and Tesla is shaping our future"** by **Ashlee Vance** bought out some inspiring and life changing thoughts for me. I share some of them which struck me the most.

1. On March 24, 2006, Falcon 1 was launched. After 25 seconds after the launch, Elon felt something wasn't right. A fire broke out in the Merlin Engine and soon after that, the rocket spun and fell directly onto the launch site.
The stats where the same for all the previous rocket companies trying to launch for the first time. Only 5 out of 9 first Pegasus launches succeeded, 3 out of 5 for Ariane, 9 out of 20 for Atlas, 9 out of 21 for Soyuz, 9 out of 18 for Proton.
Having known this Elon wrote a letter to SpaceX employees closing with "SpaceX is in this for the long haul and, come hell or high, we are going to make this work", and he did.

2. At Tesla meetings, if someone told Elon that he/she made that choice because it was the standard way things had always been done, he'd kick them out of a meeting fast.
He'd then say "I never want to hear that phrase again. What we do is hard and half-assing things won't be tolerated".
I really like the way Elon wants things to be done with a unique mindset and not how it was done in the past. This is the reason why his companies innovate and do something new all the time.

3. In 2008, things were getting tougher for Elon. Tesla was had to startover because of Roadster and SpaceX was spending too much of money for Falcon1. During a dinner, Elon said to his friend, also an investor in Tesla, "I will spend my last dollar on
these companies. If we have to move into Justine's (Elon's wife) parent's basement, we'll do it". I think Elon was always ready to risk everything he had into his companies to minimize the probability of getting bankrupt. Who does that?

4. Even when the thoughts of selling Tesla occured to Elon, he was afraid that the core values of the company would vanish if he sold it to someone else. Once, when things were really bad for Tesla, he took a loan of $40 million from SpaceX and funded the entire round himself.

5. During mid febraury of 2013, Tesla had fallen into a crisis state. People weren't buying enough cars to keep the company running. Even people who have already registered to buy were taking back their orders.
Elon made delivering cars the highest priority. He then fired senior leaders whom he deemed subpar performers and promoted a flood of junior people who had been doing above-average work.
The thing is, Elon means business. He doesn't bring personal issues into it, which has proved to be very effective for Tesla.

> Finally, Elon musk proved that one can be really passionate about something that other people think is crazy and you can really succeed.
